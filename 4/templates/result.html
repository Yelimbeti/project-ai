Melanoma

Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that originates from the pigment-producing cells called melanocytes. It is known for its potential to be aggressive and spread to other parts of the body if not detected and treated early. Melanoma often appears as an irregularly shaped.

Melanocytic Nevus

Melanocytic nevus, commonly known as a mole, is a benign (non-cancerous) skin growth that occurs when melanocytes, the cells responsible for producing skin pigment, grow in clusters or clusters in the skin. Moles can vary in appearance.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

Basal cell carcinoma is the most common form of skin cancer. It typically develops in areas of the skin that are exposed to the sun, such as the face, neck, and ears. BCC usually appears as a small, pearly or waxy bump on the skin, often with visible blood vessels.

Actinic Keratosis

Actinic keratosis, also known as solar keratosis or senile keratosis, is a pre-cancerous area of thick, scaly, or crusty skin that often feels dry or rough. It is more common in fair-skinned people and those who are frequently in the sun.

Benign Keratosis

Benign keratosis is a group of non-cancerous skin growths that often appear as raised bumps or patches on the skin. This category includes several subtypes, such as solar lentigo, seborrheic keratosis, and lichen planus-like keratosis, among others.

Dermatofibroma

Dermatofibroma is a benign skin lesion or tumor that commonly appears on the legs, although it can occur on other parts of the body as well. It is also sometimes referred to as a "histiocytoma" or "sclerosing hemangioma."

Vascular Lesion

Vascular lesions are a broad category of skin abnormalities that involve blood vessels. These lesions can occur at any age and may manifest in various forms, colors, and sizes. Vascular lesions result from abnormalities in blood vessels within the skin

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Squamous cell carcinoma (SCC) is a type of skin cancer that commonly arises from the squamous cells in the outermost layer of the skin (the epidermis). It is one of the main types of skin cancer and typically occurs in areas of the body that are frequently exposed to the sun, such as the face.
